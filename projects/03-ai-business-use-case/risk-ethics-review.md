# Risk And Ethics Review

## Key Risks

| Risk | Why It Matters | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Incomplete ratings | Missing ratings can bias conclusions about restaurant quality. | Report rating coverage and avoid ranking restaurants only on incomplete data. |
| Overstated recommendations | AI may make confident claims that are not fully supported. | Require evidence statements and human review. |
| Operational unfairness | Restaurants could be penalized based on incomplete or noisy data. | Use multiple metrics and review context before action. |
| Privacy concerns | Order data may include sensitive customer information. | Limit access, remove unnecessary identifiers, and follow data policies. |
| Automation bias | Teams may accept AI output without checking it. | Build review steps into the workflow. |

## Ethical Principles

- Be transparent about data limitations.
- Keep humans responsible for final decisions.
- Avoid using incomplete data to make unfair judgments.
- Explain recommendations in plain language.
- Monitor outcomes after decisions are made.

## Responsible AI Position

The AI assistant should support decision-making, not replace human judgment. Its purpose is to help teams notice patterns faster and communicate evidence more clearly.

# Inspiration Statement

## What I Did

I started by building an AI Product + Data Portfolio that shows structured technical skills: data analysis, AI prompt engineering, business use case design, Git workflow, and project documentation.

After creating the main portfolio structure, I wanted to add something more personal. I created a hobby widget section as a first step toward showing creativity through code. That made me think about the iPhone widgets I have seen on Pinterest: small, beautiful, personal digital spaces that feel calm, intentional, and expressive.

That became the next idea: a research section for iPhone-style widgets that combine personal hobbies, visual design, and AI-assisted product thinking.

## What Inspired Me

The inspiration came from the feeling of seeing a phone screen that looks organized, personal, and emotionally aligned with the person using it. Pinterest-style widgets often feel like small digital mood boards. They are not large applications. They are quiet, focused, and personal.

That made me wonder what it would look like to build portfolio artifacts that feel the same way.

Instead of only showing dashboards, notebooks, and documentation, I want to explore small interfaces that reflect my interests and routines. I want the portfolio to show that technical work can be functional and beautiful at the same time.

## The Heart Of The Matter

At the center of this idea is the belief that AI can help people bring their inner ideas into visible form.

Sometimes a person has a feeling before they have a plan. They know what they like, what they are drawn to, or what kind of experience they want to create, but they do not yet have the words, structure, or technical path.

AI can help with that middle space.

It can help turn inspiration into questions, questions into concepts, concepts into outlines, and outlines into working artifacts. It does not replace the person's taste or judgment. It helps give shape to something that was already there.

For me, this project is a personal ode to AI because it shows AI as a creative partner. Not a shortcut. Not a replacement. A partner that helps me organize thoughts, explore possibilities, and build something that feels connected to who I am.

## Why Widgets

Widgets are interesting because they are small. They do not try to do everything. A good widget has one job:

- remind
- summarize
- encourage
- track
- inspire
- guide a small decision

That makes widgets a good format for product thinking. They force clarity. They ask: what does the user need at a glance?

For a personal portfolio, widgets also create a bridge between hobbies and technical skills. A restaurant widget can show recommendation logic. A reading widget can show summarization. A travel widget can show planning. A wellness widget can show habit tracking. A music widget can show personalization.

## What I Want This To Become

I want this research phase to become a collection of widget concepts that feel personal, polished, and useful.

The final direction should feel like:

- personal technology with intention
- AI used thoughtfully
- design inspired by real visual references
- code that supports a clear user experience
- small artifacts that show personality and skill

This section is the beginning of that process.
